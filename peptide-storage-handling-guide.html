Step-by-Step Reconstitution Guide

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Clean surface with alcohol, wash hands thoroughly, gather supplies: bacteriostatic water, alcohol swabs, insulin syringes

2

Remove from Storage

Let peptide vial reach room temperature (10-15 minutes). Never reconstitute cold peptides as this can cause precipitation

3

Clean Vial Tops

Swab both peptide and bacteriostatic water vials with alcohol. Let dry for 30 seconds before proceeding

4

Add Bacteriostatic Water

Slowly inject water down the side of the vial, not directly onto powder. Use 1-2ml for most peptides

5

Mix Gently

Roll vial between palms or let sit for natural dissolution. NEVER shake or vortex the solution

6

Store Immediately

Place in refrigerator at 2-8¬∞C. Label with date of reconstitution. Use within recommended timeframe

Common Storage Problems & Solutions

Problem: Peptide won't dissolve completely

Causes: Too cold, wrong pH, degraded peptide

Solution: Let warm to room temperature, add water slowly, gently roll vial. If still cloudy after 30 minutes, peptide may be compromised.

Problem: Solution turned cloudy

Causes: Bacterial contamination, precipitation, degradation

Solution: Do not use. Cloudy solution indicates contamination or degradation. Dispose safely and use new vial.

Problem: Forgot to refrigerate overnight

Causes: Human error, power outage

Solution: If less than 24 hours at room temperature, most peptides remain viable. Return to refrigerator immediately. Consider potency may be slightly reduced.

Problem: Freezer failure

Causes: Power outage, equipment malfunction

Solution: Transfer to backup freezer immediately. If thawed less than 24 hours, most lyophilized peptides remain stable. Do not refreeze if fully thawed.

Storage & Handling FAQ

Q: Can I pre-load syringes for the week?

A: While possible, it's not recommended. Pre-loaded syringes can degrade faster and risk contamination. If necessary, store pre-loaded syringes in refrigerator for maximum 7 days.

Q: What's the difference between bacteriostatic water and sterile water?

A: Bacteriostatic water contains 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative, allowing multiple uses and longer stability. Sterile water has no preservatives and should be used immediately.

Q: Can I travel with reconstituted peptides?

A: Yes, use an insulated cooler with ice packs. Keep temperature between 2-8¬∞C. For air travel, pack in checked luggage with temperature monitor. Most peptides tolerate 24-48 hours at cool room temperature.

Q: How do I know if my peptide has degraded?

A: Signs include: discoloration, cloudiness, particles in solution, unusual odor, reduced effectiveness. When in doubt, don't use it. Order fresh from SigmaAudley.net.

Q: Should I filter my reconstituted peptides?

A: Not necessary for Sigma Audley peptides as they're already sterile and pure. Filtering can actually remove peptide and reduce concentration.
